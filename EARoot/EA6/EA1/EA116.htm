Why you need it: Antenna gain is crucial for extending range. You can use a high-gain (directional) antenna or a sector antenna designed for sub-6 GHz 5G frequencies.<br/>Key considerations:<br/>Directional vs. Omnidirectional:<br/>Directional antennas (e.g., patch arrays, Yagis, parabolic dishes) provide focused energy in one direction, which greatly helps achieve longer range.<br/>Omnidirectional antennas are simpler for coverage in all directions but require more transmit power to achieve the same range.<br/>Bandwidth and gain: Make sure the antenna’s rated bandwidth includes the specific 5G frequency band. Gain levels of 10–20 dBi or more are common for point-to-point or fixed wireless setups.<br/>
