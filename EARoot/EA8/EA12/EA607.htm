<b><u>Metrics</u></b><br/>A broad term that refers to any measurable value used to assess performance, progress, or outcomes.<br/><i><u>Example:</u></i> Website traffic, response time, customer complaints.<br/><br/><b><u>KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)</u></b><br/><br/>A subset of metrics that directly relate to an organization’s strategic objectives.<br/>KPIs are chosen based on what’s most critical for success.<br/><i><u>Example:</u></i> Customer retention rate, sales growth percentage, Net Promoter Score (NPS).<br/><br/><b><u>Measure of Effectiveness (MoE)</u></b><br/>Often used in military, engineering, and business strategy contexts.<br/>Focuses on how well a system, process, or strategy achieves its intended objectives.<br/>MoEs are outcome-based rather than process-based.<br/><i><u>Example:</u></i> In a military system, MoE could be “Target hit probability” or “Response time to threats.” In business, it could be “Reduction in operational costs by 20%.”<br/><br/>Metrics and KPIs are similar, but KPIs are a refined subset of metrics.<br/>MoE is different from KPIs and general metrics because it specifically measures how well something achieves its purpose, rather than just tracking performance.<br/>
