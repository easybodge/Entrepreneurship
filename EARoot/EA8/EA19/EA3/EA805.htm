Investors initially take a larger equity stake in the company (as a risk buffer), but agree to reduce their equity over time or upon the achievement of defined milestones (usually within 3–5 years).<br/><br/>��<b> Typical Structure:</b><br/>Initial terms: Investor takes X% of equity based on perceived risk and capital injected.<br/><br/>Milestone clauses: Agreement includes specific, measurable business goals — e.g.:<br/><br/><ul>
<li>Revenue thresholds</li><li>Product/tech development milestones</li><li>Team recruitment</li><li>Regulatory approvals</li><li>Strategic partnerships</li></ul>
<br/>Trigger points: When milestones are hit:<br/><br/>The investor transfers or cancels part of their equity<br/><br/>Or the founders get to buy back shares at a nominal or pre-agreed price<br/><br/>Final state: Investor retains a smaller stake if company performs well; founders retain more ownership.<br/>
