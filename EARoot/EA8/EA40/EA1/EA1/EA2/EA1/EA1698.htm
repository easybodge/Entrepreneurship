your product must offer features, functionalities, usecases that make it clearly outperform the competitors. <br/>the business model ingredients must also outperform the current competitors offerings e.g. Pricing, Channels, territory, distribution etc. Is there something about the partners, channel, resources etc that would be a game changer?<br/>Answer these questions:<br/><ul>
<li>Who are incumbents and which ones drive the market?</li><li>What is the market share of each competitor?</li><li>How many marketing and sales dollars will the market leaders spend to compete</li><li>What will the cost of entry be against incumbent competitors?</li><li>What performance attributes have customers said are important? How do competitors define performance?</li><li>What share of this market does the company want to capture in the first three years?</li><li>How do the competitors define the market?</li><li>Are there existing standards? if so, whose agenda is driving the standards?</li><li>Does the company seek to embrace these standards, extend them, or replace them?</li><li>how have existing competitors defined the basis of competition? Is it in terms of product attributes? Service? What are their claims? Features?What makes the new company and its product stand out as dramatically different? features? performance? price?</li></ul>
<br/>Is the company entering an existing market, re-segmenting an existing market, creating a new market or cloning a market?<br/>Market type drives marketing spending, sales timing and cash need. <br/>For existing market with dominant market player, segment existing market to create submarket where your product is unique or substantially differnet. Or create a new market, you can define a space the market leader doesn't address at all. <br/>Remeber the cost of head-on attack: 3 times the budget of the market leader or 1.7times in ununstable market with no major market leader but maximum share of about 25% from the biggest market player. <br/><br/><br/>
