If after looking at data, you can't find other companies with comparable products? Congratulations- you are creating a new market. <br/>As there is no market-share battles with competitors, there are also no existing customers. <br/>If there are no existing customers, even an indifinite demand-creation budget at the point of product launch won't garner market share. Creating new market is about long term customer education and adoption. <br/>Startups creating new markets won't create a market of sufficient size to generate a profit until three to seven years from product launch. This sobering piece of data is derived from looking at the results of hundreds of high-tech startups of the last 20 years. <br/>Answer these questions:<br/><ul>
<li>What are markets adjacent to new one being created?</li><li>What markets will potential customers come from?</li><li>What's the company vision and why will lots of people care</li><li>What 'never could hve been done before' will make customers use/buy?</li><li>How long will it take to educate potential customers to grow a market of sufficient size? what size is that?</li><li>How will company educate the market? How ill it create the demand?</li><li>Given that no customers yet exist, what are realistic sales forecasts for the first three years?</li><li>How much financing will it take to soldier on while educating and growing the market?</li><li>What will stop  a well-heeled competitor from taking over the market once the startup develops it? This phenomenon is the source of the phrase 'pioneers are the ones with arrows in their backs'</li><li>Is the product better-defined as one that will re-segment a market or enter an existing one?</li></ul><p/>
