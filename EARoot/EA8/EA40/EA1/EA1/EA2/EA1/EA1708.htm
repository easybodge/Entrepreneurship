Is company entering an existing market, resegmenting an existing market, creating a new market or cloning a market?<br/>Market type drives marketing spending, sales timing and cash need<br/><br/>Be careful with distinction between new and existing market, if market is new, its means there are no existing customers or competitors<br/><br/>Its easy to understand market type in terms of 'class of products'. When entering market that has tons of competitors, whether its smartphones, social networks, glucose meters, aireplanes, the choice has already been made for you.. its an existing market. If company invents a new class of product no one has ever seen before, its more likely to be a new market. <br/><br/>Would some part of existing market buy a product designed to address its specific needs? then think re-segmented market. Re-segmented market is when startup carve out a distinct segment of existing market by reframing value in a way that incumbents are not serving well. e.g. A startup targets a specific segment that incumbent overlook. they differentiate with unique features and focus that matter only to that segment. e.g. Testla didn't try to beat Toyota on mass-market cars. Instead, it re-segmented the auto market by focusing on wealthy, eco-conscious early adopters who wanted luxury electric vehicles. <br/><br/>or are there customers in existing market who will buy a product that offers cheaper, better, faster alternative to existing product, then again its an existing market<br/><br/>Countries like China, Japan, India, Russia etc have large local market. Can we adopt/borrow/copy an already successful business model from US and adapt it to local language in these countries that its a Clone Market. <br/><br/><br/><br/><br/>
