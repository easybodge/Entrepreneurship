A steady stream of product upgrades and enhancements should always keep the product ahead of competition. These are core-value creation activities and should be address in the value proposition hypothesis. <br/><br/>Strengthen and bolster retention. This can be activities such as outbound calls to ensure they are happy or complex as a multi-tiered loyalty-points programs to reward repeat purchases. Customer loyalty programs such as points, rewards, discounts as well as long-term retention incentives like multi-year contracts. <br/><br/>Are there things partners can do or other resources in business model?<br/><br/>Send Product update bulletins: Create small tip sheets or user notes on how customers are making the most of the product. Send them to all users. <br/><br/>Customer lock-in/high switching costs: Consider tactics for locking in customers to your product or solution through long-term contracts, unique technology or data that can't be easily transferred. <br/><br/>For mobile/app based products, mobile/app push notifications give developers the ability to push messages to users even when the app is closed. <br/><br/>Try to build some kind of retention metrics mechanism in app/website to measure signs of dwindling visits, page views, increased time between visits, average customer life, lifetime value, increase in complaints, help or support tickets<br/><br/><br/>its 5-10X cheapter to keep a customer than to acquire one. <br/><br/><br/>
