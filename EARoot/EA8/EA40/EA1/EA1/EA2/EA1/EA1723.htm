Steve Blank says a market = a set of companies with common attributes.<br/>In the figure (KnowWonder in the center), the markets around it are the four ovals. Each oval = one set of companies that share common attributes.<br/><br/><b><u>1. Support Software</u></b><br/><br/>Companies: Remedy, Scopus, Clarify<br/><br/>Common Attributes: Enterprise-grade customer support systems.<br/><br/>Attribute = built for help desks / support centers.<br/><br/>Attribute = sold to large organizations, usually B2B.<br/><br/>Attribute = focus on ticketing, case management, customer support workflow.<br/><br/><b><u>2. Help Tools</u></b><br/><br/>Companies: Logitear, eHelp, Microsoft<br/><br/>Common Attributes: Tools for self-help, FAQs, and customer training.<br/><br/>Attribute = lightweight, used directly by end-users.<br/><br/>Attribute = focus on enabling customers to help themselves rather than relying on human support.<br/><br/>Attribute = software utilities often integrated into websites or apps.<br/><br/><b><u>3. Consumers</u></b><br/><br/>Companies: Intuit, Lotus<br/><br/>Common Attributes: Consumer-focused software products.<br/><br/>Attribute = easy to use for non-technical individuals.<br/><br/>Attribute = mass-market, not enterprise-specific.<br/><br/>Attribute = aimed at personal productivity, financial management, or consumer usability.<br/><br/><b><u>4. Service Providers</u></b><br/><br/>Companies: Bracket, OneTwo, EDS<br/><br/>Common Attributes: Outsourced service providers.<br/><br/>Attribute = companies that deliver customer support as a service.<br/><br/>Attribute = rely less on software products, more on people + process.<br/><br/>Attribute = used by enterprises that don’t want to manage support in-house.<br/><br/><b><u>✅ So in summary:</u></b><br/><br/>Set of companies with common attributes = each oval category.<br/><br/>Attributes = what they all have in common (type of solution, customer type, business model).<br/><br/>Companies = examples listed inside each oval.<br/>
