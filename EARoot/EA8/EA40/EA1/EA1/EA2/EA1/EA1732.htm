For physical products:<br/><ul>
<li>Distributors(route to Dealers or resellers or mass merchants)-- they are expensive but provide personal attention and can showcase and promote products they like.</li><li>Dealers or retailers - they take almost twice the markup on the wholesale price. They take in small quantities but provide personal attention and can showcase /promote products they like.</li><li>Mass merchandisers e.g. Walmart, Sainsburys - very expensive often charging 50% of retail price for carrying product. They require 'test market' prior launching new products. Benefit is massive distribution and advertising/marketing potential. Weakness is long lead tme to sell into.</li><li>Direct sales force</li><li>value-added reseller</li><li>system integrators</li><li>OEM- buys product to make bigger product such as pc maker buying PCIe cards or gpus. Drawback is loos of brand as it becomes used up as OEM bigger product. Strength is massive volume potential</li><li>web/online</li></ul>
<br/>one of the best strategy is to hire 'Independent sales-rep firms'  those who only charge commission they have custoemr base and can serve as conduit. It is a real fast way to get national distribution at a variable cost with little upfront spending. weakness is they are loyal to their customer not to you but will sell fast.<br/><br/>Most important lesson is do not launch product, hire sales force, look for attractive channels until market validation is completed. Company is still testing and expanding its hypothesis and needs to focus on learning. Don't launch via sales force, chain stores and direct mail all at once!<br/>The smartest move is to observe existing buying patterns and habits for similar products and product categories, since customers are demonstrating their preferred channel by spending their money there. <br/><br/>For web/mobile products:<br/>Success in web based product is almost entirely dependent on creating demand, attracting visitors to site cost-effectively and persuading them to engage with the product or buy it. Channels are:<br/>Dedicated e-commerce (your website)<br/><ul>
<li>Platform App store such as play store and apple store</li><li>2-Step distribution (Amazon, CDW) - this is a traditional amazon distribution where they provide fulfillment. downside is them taking 55% of retail price to sell. Strength is fastest way to get national distribution</li><li>Aggregator (Lendingtree, Zappos) - online equivalent of physical shopping mall usually in single category. For instance, hundreds of mortgges and financial products are available through LendingTree.com</li><li>Social Commercie (Facebook, Twitter) - </li><li>Flash Sales (Groupon) - offer a list of consumers deep discounts on branded merchansdise with offers expiring in 24-48 hours. Groupon assemble massive e-mail and social networking lists of consumers eager to obtain product discounts in specific categories or geographies. This is again great for fast, potentially massive distribution. Great for product launces but often painfully expensive. End users often expect 50% discounts from retail price. </li></ul><p/>
