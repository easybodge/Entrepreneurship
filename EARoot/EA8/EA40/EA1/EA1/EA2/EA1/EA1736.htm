Re-segmented market is when startup carve out a distinct segment of existing market by reframing value in a way that incumbents are not serving well. e.g. A startup targets a specific segment that incumbent overlook. they differentiate with unique features and focus that matter only to that segment. e.g. Testla didn't try to beat Toyota on mass-market cars. Instead, it re-segmented the auto market by focusing on wealthy, eco-conscious early adopters who wanted luxury electric vehicles. <br/><br/><ul>
<li>Any company can take customers away from any other company - if it can define the battle. When re-segmenting an existing market, positioning rests on either:</li><li>Finding a unique niche(market segment) where some product feature or service redefines the market, creating a clear competitive advantage.</li><li>being the low-cost provider or</li><li>combining differentiation and lower operating cost into a Blue Ocean Strategy that creates an uncontested market space and makes the competition irrelevant while creating and capturing new demand.</li></ul>
Answer the following for existing markets with dominance from single or two players:<br/><ul>
<li>What existing markets are customers coming from?</li><li>What are the unique characteristics of those customers?</li><li>What compelling needs of those customers are unmet by existing suppliers?</li><li>What compelling product features will get customers to abandon their current suppliers?</li><li>Why couldn't existing companies offer the same thing?</li><li>How long will it take you to grow a market of sufficient size? What size?</li><li>How will company educate the market and create demand?</li><li>Given that no customers yet exist in the new segment, what are realistic sales forecasts?</li><li>How can this forecast be tested?</li><li>Can parts of business model be changed to differentiate the company?</li></ul><p/>
