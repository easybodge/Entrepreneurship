<li>Review all the customer feedback report cards, summaries, and analysis—there should be quite a pile of it. Here again, as with validating the problem question, be sure there’s robust enthusiasm for the product, even in its MVP form. </li><li>Look for comments like “I have to have this now” or “Everyone I know will want one” or “When can I get it?” Lukewarm or average enthusiasm seldom delivers scalable startups. Be objective and park entrepreneurial enthusiasm at the door.</li><li></li><li>Did the minimum viable product generate significant “buy” or “engage” enthusiasm? </li><li>Was the product exciting enough to stimulate significant customer referrals? </li><li>Did those referred customers buy or engage at an encouraging rate? </li><li>Did the long-term product vision generate highly positive customer feedback? Is the latest product-features-and-benefits hypothesis still appropriate and profitable? </li><li>Did the long-term product-delivery schedule generate purchase enthusiasm?</li></ul><p/>
