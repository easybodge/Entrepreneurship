Web/mobile businesses focus on data collection, analysis and optimization from the day they go live till the day the doors close. A web/mobile startup’s conversion funnel monitors customers through their entire life cycle. The entire process is driven by a never-ending campaign to test, measure, and optimize every step in the funnel, from awareness to purchase. It’s a 24/7 process of “try it, measure it, tweak it.<br/><br/>Web/mobile businesses focus on data collection, analysis and optimization from the day they go live til the day the doors close.<br/><br/>The products you build should be instrumented to measure every click on the website or app, its source, and the action it causes or doesn’t. As a result, the management team should have at its fingertips a dashboard that summarizes every key, actionable user behavior, and delivers insights and trends that drive continual business improvement. <br/><ul>
<li>There are two parts to building a metrics tool set: determining which key business metrics need to be measured</li><li>developing a dashboard or system to collect and monitor the data</li></ul>
<br/>Using customer relationship hypothesis, identify the metrics for success in business model. <br/><br/><ul>
<li>How many customers are acquired, and how many of those are activated? (And how many are lost? And where in the purchase process do you lose them?)</li><li>How fast do they arrive and how fast do they activate? After one page view or visit, or 20 of either?</li><li>How much does each acquisition and activation cost?</li><li>How good are the customers who are being acquired? Active users/spenders who return again and again or visitors with no “stickiness” at all?</li></ul>
<br/>For consistency’s sake, consider organizing the metrics the way the sales funnel is organized—by acquisition and activation metrics. For example: <br/>Acquisition metrics: <br/><ul>
<li>Total number of visits by visitor type, time of day, and source, and page views per visit</li><li>Paid and referred traffic conversion rates by source (how many links or banners, and at what cost, to generate one visitor or user) and cost per acquired/activated user</li><li>Quantities and percentages of referred traffic by source</li><li>Unique behaviors or actions of subsets or segments of the customer universe I know I’m wasting half of my ad budget. I only wish I knew which half…</li><li>Activation metrics</li><li>Total number of activations: another percentage of hourly/daily/weekly acquisitions</li><li>Number/percentage of activations, tracked back to the original source</li><li>Number/percentage/cost per activation, based on quality (heavy users and big spenders or modest spenders and inactive users?), wherever identifiable, preferably by source</li><li>Number of visits, page views, referrals per activated user, by source and by cost</li><li>Your startup’s future lies in its customer acquisition, activation and retention data. This is so important that many web businesses actually display their real-time site-performance data on a jumbo flat-screen monitor in the office so that it’s always the center of attention. Off-the-shelf dashboard systems are available, and home-grown solutions are readily built using tools as simple as Excel.</li></ul><p/>
