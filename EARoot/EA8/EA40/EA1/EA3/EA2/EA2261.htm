<b>NPS — Net Promoter Score</b><br/><ul>
<li><b>Question:</b> <i>“How likely are you to recommend our product/service to a friend or colleague?”</i> (0–10 scale).</li><li><b>Scoring:</b><ul>
<li>9–10 = Promoters</li><li>7–8 = Passives</li><li>0–6 = Detractors</li></ul>
</li><br/><li><b>Formula:</b> NPS = %Promoters – %Detractors (range: –100 to +100).</li><br/><li><b>Purpose:</b> Measures <b>loyalty</b> and word-of-mouth potential.</li><br/></ul>
<br/><b>��</b><b> CSAT — Customer Satisfaction Score</b><br/><ul>
<li><b>Question:</b> <i>“How satisfied are you with [product/service/experience]?”</i> (usually 1–5 stars, or Very Dissatisfied → Very Satisfied).</li><li><b>Formula:</b> CSAT = (% of satisfied customers, e.g. 4 or 5 stars).</li><li><b>Purpose:</b> Measures <b>immediate satisfaction</b> with a product, feature, or support interaction.</li></ul>
<br/><b>��</b><b> CES — Customer Effort Score</b><br/><ul>
<li><b>Question:</b> <i>“How easy was it to accomplish [task] with our product/service?”</i> (typically 1–7 scale, from Very Difficult → Very Easy).</li><li><b>Purpose:</b> Measures <b>ease of use</b> and friction in customer journeys (onboarding, support, checkout). Lower effort usually = higher retention.</li></ul><p/>
