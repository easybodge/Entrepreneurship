Let’s walk through a detailed numerical example.<br/><br/><b>First Investment Round</b><br/><br/>Assumptions:<br/>Pre-money valuation: £500,000<br/>Investment amount: £100,000<br/>Pre-money shares: Assume the company has 500,000 shares (implying £1 per share).<br/><br/>Calculations:<br/>Shares Issued to Investor:<br/>With £100,000 invested at £1 per share, the investor receives 100,000 shares.<br/>Post-money Total Shares:<br/>500,000 (existing) + 100,000 (new) = 600,000 shares.<br/>Investor’s Ownership Percentage (Round 1):<br/>100,000 / 600,000 ≈ 16.67%<br/><br/><br/><b>Second Investment Round (2 Years Later)</b><br/><br/>New Round Assumptions:<br/>Pre-money valuation: £2,000,000<br/>Investment amount: £1,000,000<br/>Shares Pre-Round 2: The company still has 600,000 shares from the first round.<br/><br/>Determine New Share Price:<br/>New share price = Pre-money valuation / Pre-round 2 shares<br/>£2,000,000 / 600,000 = £3.33 per share (approximately).<br/><br/>New Shares Issued:<br/>Number of new shares = Investment amount / New share price<br/>£1,000,000 / £3.33 ≈ 300,000 shares.<br/><br/>Post-money Total Shares After Round 2:<br/>600,000 (existing) + 300,000 (new) = 900,000 shares.<br/>Investor’s Ownership After Second Round:<br/><br/>Their number of shares remains at 100,000 (assuming no anti-dilution or other adjustments).<br/>New percentage = 100,000 / 900,000 ≈ 11.11%<br/><br/>What Does the First Investor "Get"?<br/>At the Time of Their Investment:<br/>They received 100,000 shares, which represented 16.67% of the company immediately post-investment.<br/><br/>After the Second Round:<br/>Their 100,000 shares now represent about 11.11% of the enlarged share pool.<br/><br/>Value of Their Stake:<br/>With the new share price of about £3.33 (derived from a post-money valuation of £3,000,000 after Round 2), the value of their stake is:<br/>100,000 shares × £3.33 ≈ £333,000.<br/><br/><b>Summary</b><br/>Initial Ownership: 16.67% immediately after the first round.<br/>Ownership After 2 Years and Second Round: Approximately 11.11%, assuming no additional share issuance to protect their percentage (i.e., without anti-dilution adjustments).<br/>Thus, while the investor’s percentage ownership is diluted from 16.67% to 11.11%, the increase in the company’s valuation (from a post-money valuation of £600,000 to £3,000,000) means the value of their shares has increased significantly from their initial £100,000 investment.<br/>
