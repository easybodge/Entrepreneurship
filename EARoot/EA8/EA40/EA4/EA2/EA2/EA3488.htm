Below is a step‑by‑step look at how a UK venture‑capital firm actually locks down the money it later invests in start‑ups.<br/>Think of it as a funnel that moves from crafting a story → getting “yes, in principle” → signing legally binding commitments → calling the cash when you need it.<br/><br/>1  Lay the Groundwork – Why this fund, why you?<br/><br/>What LPs look for What you must prepare<br/>Proven ability to source & exit deals (track‑record table, DPI/IRR) Deal & exit case studies, audited performance numbers<br/>Clear, differentiated thesis (stage, sector, geography) 2‑page teaser + 15‑slide deck<br/>Skin‑in‑the‑game (1‑2 % GP commitment) Personal capital, recycled carry<br/>Operational infrastructure & custodian Budget, compliance policies, service‑provider list<br/>Most managers wrap these items into a Private Placement Memorandum (PPM), a draft Limited‑Partnership Agreement (LPA) and an ILPA‑style Due‑Diligence Questionnaire (DDQ). ILPA publishes templates and the governance/fee norms LPs expect <br/>ILPA<br/>.<br/><br/>2  Choose the Right Vehicle & Regulatory Wrapper<br/>Fund domicile – most UK GPs still use an English Limited Partnership or, for pan‑European LP bases, a Luxembourg SCSp/Jersey/Guernsey parallel.<br/><br/>Regulation – the management company must be an AIFM under the UK’s AIFMD regime; first‑time managers often rent a “host‑AIFM” until they obtain full FCA authorisation. The FCA is currently consulting on lighter rules for alternatives to spur competition <br/>FCA<br/>.<br/><br/>Entities –<br/><br/>GP (carries the 20 % carry)<br/><br/>Fund LP (blind‑pool where investor commitments sit)<br/><br/>Management Company (receives the 2 % management fee, employs staff).<br/><br/>3  Line‑up Anchor LPs & Run the Roadshow<br/><br/>Typical timeline Key actions & tools<br/>Months 0‑3 – Pre‑marketing “Friendly” LPs review draft docs (allowed under AIFMD pre‑marketing rules); CRM like Affinity plus DocSend data‑room links.<br/>Months 4‑9 – Formal marketing Roadshow, on‑site ODD, track‑record verification. Placement agents (e.g., Rede Partners) can open doors but charge ~2 % + retainer.<br/>Month 9 – First close Minimum target raised (often ≥ 30 % of fund size). LPs sign Subscription Agreements and the LPA; capital stays un‑drawn.<br/>Month 15 – Final close Hard‑cap reached, fundraising shuts.<br/>Cornerstone capital makes everything easier. In the UK the British Business Bank can act as an anchor through its Enterprise Capital Fund programme, which explicitly lowers barriers for first‑time managers <br/>British Business Bank<br/>. BBB is already the single largest LP in UK VC <br/>bvca.co.uk<br/>.<br/><br/>4  Capital Commitments → Capital Calls<br/>LP commitments are contractual, not wired upfront.<br/><br/>The GP issues capital‑call notices (ILPA provides the template and reporting fields) giving LPs 10 – 15  business days to wire their pro‑rata share <br/>ILPA<br/>.<br/><br/>Calls average 10‑25 % of commitments in year 1, then quarterly or deal‑by‑deal through the 4‑ to 5‑year investment period.<br/><br/>Many funds maintain a subscription‑line of credit with a bank (secured on LP commitments) so they can close deals quickly and batch calls monthly. ILPA guidance recommends using these lines sparingly and disclosing the cost to LPs <br/>ILPA<br/>.<br/><br/>5  Deploy, Monitor, Exit, Distribute<br/>Invest – within the LPA’s mandate.<br/><br/>Report – quarterly ILPA template (portfolio valuation, fees, ESG KPIs).<br/><br/>Governance – Limited‑Partner Advisory Committee (LPAC) approves conflicts, key‑person waivers, bridge‑loan usage.<br/><br/>Exits & waterfall – once all capital + preferred return (usually 8 %) is returned to LPs, the carry kicks in; ILPA’s “whole‑of‑fund” waterfall is market standard <br/>ILPA<br/>.<br/><br/>Recycling – proceeds may be recycled during the investment period if allowed in the LPA.<br/><br/>Wind‑up – target life 10 years + two 1‑year extensions.<br/><br/>6  Digital Infrastructure That Makes Fundraising & Calls Smoother<br/><br/>Function Common SaaS<br/>LP CRM & pipeline Affinity, Salesforce Private‑Equity Cloud<br/>Data‑room / doc analytics DocSend, Dropbox DocSend, Carta DataRooms<br/>E‑subs & KYC onboarding Anduin, Passthrough, Flow, iCapital<br/>Fund accounting & investor portal Carta, Allvue, Fundwave<br/>Call/Distribution automation Dynamo, Hazeltree, ILPA template plug‑ins<br/>Regulatory hosting AIFM service platforms (e.g., IQ‑EQ, Langham Hall)<br/>7  Special UK Angles to Consider<br/>SEIS/EIS & VCT wrappers – retail tax‑advantaged structures; can provide early “retail” capital but limit ticket size and investment scope.<br/><br/>Enterprise Capital Funds – Government matches private money up to a 2:1 ratio, capping BBB’s downside so LPs see asymmetric upside — ideal for sub‑£100 m first funds <br/>British Business Bank<br/>.<br/><br/>FCA 2025 strategy – more scrutiny on private‑markets valuation, conflicts and LP disclosures; make sure policies are robust before you market <br/>Bovill Newgate<br/>.<br/><br/>Quick Checklist Before You Start Dialling LPs<br/>Anchor identified? ≥ 10 % of fund size.<br/><br/>GP commit secured? Personal cash or rolled carry.<br/><br/>LPA & DDQ drafted and benchmarked to ILPA norms.<br/><br/>Regulatory status (host‑AIFM or FCA variation‑of‑permission) confirmed.<br/><br/>Auditor, administrator, depositary appointed.<br/><br/>Data room live with track record, pipeline, ESG policy, fee model.<br/><br/>Tick these boxes and you’ll have the credibility—and the legal plumbing—to convert those warm LP conversations into real pounds you can deploy into start‑ups. Good luck raising Fund I!<br/>
