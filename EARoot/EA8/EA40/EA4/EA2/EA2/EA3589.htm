<b>Uncapped:</b><br/><font color="#cd0000">– </font>converts into equity at the value of the next priced<br/>round <b>regardless </b>of the valuation of the round.<br/><br/>Uncapped Convertible Notes<br/><br/>Definition: An uncapped convertible note does not have a valuation cap. The conversion into equity is determined solely by other conversion terms, typically a discount rate.<br/>How It Works:<br/>When the note converts, the conversion price is calculated using the discount rate applied to the valuation in the subsequent financing round.<br/>For example, if the discount rate is 20% and the startup raises funds at a £10 million valuation, the note converts at an effective valuation of £8 million (i.e., 20% less), which determines the number of shares the investor receives.<br/>Consideration:<br/>Investors bear more risk with an uncapped note since they don’t have the additional upside protection that a cap provides. If the valuation in the next round is extremely high, the investor may receive a smaller percentage of equity compared to what they would have received under a capped arrangement.<br/><br/>
