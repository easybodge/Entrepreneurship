Offer two/three options for terms of financing<br/><br/>When finalizing an investment round, you might present investors with several distinct financing options. This approach allows them to choose terms that best suit their investment strategy. For example, you could offer:<br/><br/><b>1. Convertible Note</b><br/><i><u>Mechanism:</u></i> The investor’s money converts into equity at a future valuation milestone, such as the next financing round or upon reaching a revenue threshold.<br/>Terms:<br/><i><u>Time Horizon: </u></i>Typically maturing in 12–24 months.<br/><i><u>Repayment:</u></i> If conversion doesn’t occur by maturity, interest accrues, and repayment may be required.<br/>Investor ROI Example: An 8% annual interest with a 20% discount on conversion can result in a larger share of the company if it grows rapidly.<br/><br/><b>2. SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)</b><br/><i><u>Mechanism:</u></i> The investor provides funds now in exchange for the right to acquire equity in the future.<br/>Terms:<br/><i><u>Threshold: </u></i>Conversion happens at the next priced round.<br/><i><u>Time Horizon:</u></i> No fixed maturity date.<br/>Investor ROI Example: With a valuation cap, an investor might secure equity at a lower valuation, increasing their percentage ownership if the company’s value significantly rises.<br/><br/><b>3. Revenue-Based Financing</b><br/><i><u>Mechanism:</u></i> Investors receive a fixed percentage of monthly revenues until a predetermined multiple of their initial investment is repaid.<br/>Terms:<br/><i><u>Time Horizon:</u></i> Often structured over 3–5 years.<br/><i><u>Repayment</u></i>: Payments are made as a portion of revenue, not fixed periodic payments.<br/>Investor ROI Example: If the startup grows quickly, repayments accelerate, providing a faster return; if growth is steady, the investor still enjoys a consistent income stream.<br/><b>4. Equity Investment via Preferred or Common Stock</b><br/><b><i><u>Preferred Stock:</u></i></b><br/><i><u>Mechanism: </u></i>Investors buy equity at a specific valuation from the outset, with additional rights and protections such as liquidation preferences, anti-dilution provisions, and dividend rights.<br/>Investor ROI Example: These protections mean that in a liquidity event (e.g., sale or IPO), preferred shareholders often recover their investment first and might receive a higher return relative to their investment compared to common shareholders.<br/><b><i><u>Common Stock:</u></i></b><br/><i><u>Mechanism:</u></i> Investors buy a stake in the company, generally without the additional rights attached to preferred stock.<br/>Investor ROI Example: While common stock might offer a higher upside in a successful exit, it usually comes with higher risk since investors receive their returns after preferred shareholders have been compensated.<br/>By providing multiple options, you demonstrate flexibility and readiness to accommodate different investor preferences. The choice between instruments like convertible notes or SAFEs versus direct equity investments (preferred or common stock) depends on your company’s current stage, valuation certainty, and the level of risk both parties are willing to accept.<br/>
