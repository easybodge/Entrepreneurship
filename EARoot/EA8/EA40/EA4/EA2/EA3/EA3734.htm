<ul>
<li>one share is equal to fractional ownership of company. 1000/10000 shares in 1/10s of company ownership. </li><li>generally issued to founders of company. Also to employees joining management.</li><li>easiest and simple and least costly. All shareholders have same rights and previlige.</li><li>Value is diminshed when preferred stock are issued. Not convertible into other securities. </li><li>Most junior -- last person to get money on liquidation</li><li>each share has one vote on corporate matters.</li><li>Dividends are payable but only when declared by board and preferred get preference over common stock holders. </li><li>Enough shares should be authorized to allow awards for key people</li><li>Vesting schedules should be established. for instance, stay 4 year in company to keep common stocks when you leave.</li><li>Preemptive right to keep their prorata share in the company. </li><li>Par value - value at which founders buy shares. price below which share can't be sold.Should be set very low (e.g. $0.0001). This can be very important, in the event business fails and legal authorities get involved then founders have to pay par-value to get clear. So lower the value, lower the liability. </li></ul><p/>
