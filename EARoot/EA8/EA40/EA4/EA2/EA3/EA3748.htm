<li>Equity with some debt characteristics. </li><li>Fixed or adjustable dividend normally fixed paid over common shareholders. </li><li> Preferred by VCs. </li><li>Carry voting and convertibility rights</li><li>Converts into common at holder's election. </li><li>Automatically converts on a Qualified Initial Public Offering. </li><li>May include pay to play provision. get preference to participate in future rounds. </li><li>Excercised in liquidation e.g. sale of company (preferred over common). </li><li>Standard liquidation - investor receives the greater of a) the original purchase plus accured dividends or b) percentage of the proceeds on an 'as-converted' basis. </li><li>Anti-dilution protection. Protect the investment if company issues stock at lower price. 1) Full rachet - can be risky to the investor if company goes on to raise funding at reduced share parice. 2) Weighted Average is preferred option and protects investor. </li><li>Preferred stocks have redemption rights. they can get Original Purchase Price + all accrued dividents. </li><li>Enables investors to get liquidity after 4-6 years in a walking dead scenario i.e. company is not going to liquidate and has not created enought value to do IP or become an acquisition targer. </li><li>they have voting rights. </li><li>Votes as a class in certain circumstances i.e. liquidating company, merger, creating new lass of preferred stock. </li><li>Contain investor rights agreement. 1) Board Representation. They get seat on board of directors. 2) Information Rights. Monthly/quarterly financial, audited statements, access to review company's books. 3) Preemptive Rights. To maintain percentage ownership by purchasign prorate portion. 4) Protective Provisions. Prevent certain transactions unless preferred board representative approve. 5) First Refusal/Co-sale. limits the transferability of the share of founders. 6) Registration rights. Register sale of investor's restricted stock. </li><li>Each subsequenct issuance of preferred stock(Class A, B, C) has preference over the previous class. </li></ul><p/>
